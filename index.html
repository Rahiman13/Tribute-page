About Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Missile Man of India, was a visionary scientist, educator and statesman who left an indelible mark on India's space and missile programs.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, also known as the Missile Man of India, was a renowned scientist and the 11th President of India. He played a pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam's contributions to science and technology are immense. Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam inspired millions with his vision, wisdom, and humility. His dedication to the youth of the nation and his relentless pursuit of knowledge made him a beloved figure in India and beyond.

Awards & Achievements

11th President of India

Kalam was elected as the 11th president of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the then-opposition Indian National Congress. Widely referred to as the "People's President",[6] he returned to his civilian life of education, writing and public service after a single term.

Padma Bhushan

APJ Abdul Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 1981 for his contributions to the Indian space program and his work as a scientist. At that time, he was serving as the Director of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)'s Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) project.

Padma Vibhushan

APJ Abdul Kalam received India's second-highest civilian honor, the Padma Vibhushan, in 1990, acknowledging his scientific service and pivotal role in developing the nation's ballistic missile and space launch vehicle technology. His leadership in defense research, particularly in missile technology, garnered widespread recognition and acclaim.

Bharat Ratna

APJ Abdul Kalam was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997 for his exceptional contributions to scientific research, technological advancements, and his role in shaping India's defense capabilities, notably in missile technology. His dedication to national progress and inspiring leadership made him a deserving recipient.

Veer Savarkar Award

APJ Abdul Kalam received the Veer Savarkar Award in recognition of his significant contributions to national development, particularly in science, technology, and education. His relentless pursuit of excellence and his visionary leadership made him a fitting recipient of this prestigious honor.

Contributions

APJ Abdul Kalam contributed significantly to the Agni series, primarily through his leadership and guidance in missile development programs. As the former Director of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later as the Scientific Advisor to the Defence Minister, Kalam played a pivotal role in advancing India's missile technology. His vision and expertise contributed to the successful development and enhancement of various Agni missiles, strengthening India's strategic capabilities in defense and deterrence.

Former Director of DRDO

APJ Abdul Kalam played a significant role in the development and advancement of India's Prithvi series of missiles. As a prominent scientist and leader in India's defense research sector, Kalam provided crucial guidance and expertise that contributed to the design, testing, and deployment of the Prithvi missiles. His contributions helped enhance India's strategic defense capabilities and bolster its deterrence posture.

Instrumental leadership

APJ Abdul Kalam contributed expertise and leadership to the development of the Trishul missile system, enhancing India's indigenous defense capabilities. His guidance bolstered the research and technological innovations crucial for the successful deployment of the Trishul missile, marking a milestone in India's defense program.
